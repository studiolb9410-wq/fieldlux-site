§ 01 · Judgment Engine

Auto-verdict on every install.

The analyzer reads your projector choice, lens configuration, and target geometry, then delivers one of three answers:

READY · ships within mount/drift tolerance
AT_RISK · works on a best-case day, no margin
NOT_RECOMMENDED · expected to fail on a measurement that matters

Behind every verdict is a transparent set of install checks: brightness margin, contrast, pixel density, coverage gap, spill, blend hotspot, and more. Underlying standards and pass/fail rationales are documented in § 05. Every failing metric shows both the reason and the recommended fix.

§ 03 · Prescription Engine

Concrete actions — not just warnings.

When brightness fails, we don't say "low lux". We say:

Move all 3 projectors 40 cm closer as a group
    — or —
upgrade to 15,000 lm units
    — or —
swap ELPLM15 for short-throw ELPLU04.

Distance and lumens prescriptions are derived from the actual physics — inverse-square law, lens-throw equations, blend-footprint geometry. Equipment-swap suggestions and conflict resolution fall back to documented heuristics, traceable in the prescription card.

Conflicting prescriptions ("closer for brightness" vs "back for coverage") are detected and reconciled automatically. The real trade-off is surfaced so you understand why one path wins.

§ 05 · Assumptions & Error Budget

No black box.
Every assumption is public.

Surface reflectance, projector uniformity (ANSI 9-point), P20 sampling grid, stress reserve floor, non-planar fallback method — every value, every source, every error budget is surfaced in the analysis panel and the PDF export.

Surface reflectance (default) 0.60 Δ ±0.10
Projector uniformity ANSI 9-point Δ ±5%
P20 sampling 16×16 Δ ±5%
Non-planar fallback bbox centroid Δ ±15~30%
Indirect bounce 1-bounce form factor Δ ±50%

Defend every verdict with the exact physics citation. Schema is version-frozen (v1); any change triggers a contract-test failure to prevent silent drift.

§ 06 · Multi-target Analysis

Analyze what you actually project onto.

Flat surfaces, room walls, imported 3D building models, photographic targets. The same judgment engine runs on all four. Import glTF, FBX, OBJ, Collada. Lens-warped projection lands on real mesh geometry — with a documented bbox-centroid fallback when ray-mesh intersection misses (error budget disclosed in § 05).
